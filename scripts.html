Elements of a Ceremony Scripts

Every ceremony I write is unique to each couple! I have some suggested outlines below, and examples of what you may want to include. Should we work together, I have sample scripts that we can pull from, and of course we will write in your own story, vows, and elements as desired! You can also incorporate other aspects including an audience vow, ring warming, or other unique activity to unite your bond in marriage. While I don't perform strictly religious ceremonies, I am happy to incorporate traditional or cultural elements into your ceremony. This is your day and your promises to enter marraige, so everything that is said will be custom to your vision.

Example Elements

Introduction and No Phone Announcement - I would recommend this before your ceremony procession. Welcome - welcoming the guests and, perhaps more importantly, you to your wedding! Reading - Invite a family member or friend to select their own or I can incorporate a reading of your choice. Love Story - We can craft a beatiful telling of your love story to share with the guests. I suggest couples write their own since you know your love story best! Unity Activity: Ring Warming, Rope Braiding, Sand Blending, or another activity to signify the bond you're committing to Audience Vows - You can choose to engage the guests as the important people in your life to take a vow to continue to support you in your married life The I Dos and Vows - You can write your own vows and read them here, or we can create ones that represent your values and promises to each other that I will read to you Ring Exchange - Exchange rings as a symbol of your committment and love! Promouncement - You're married! Kiss! I will complete the marriage license after the ceremony and, if you're in a state where witnesses are needed, they will need to sign after the ceremony as well. We'll discuss who you may want those people to be and coordinate with them so they can sign and enjoy the celebration!

Vow Renewal

Celebrating years of love and the promises you've made, kept, and wish to reaffirm. A vow renewal can take any elements from a wedding ceremony but may be more focused on your journey together, the love you've built, and any promises and vows you wish to re-commit to.
